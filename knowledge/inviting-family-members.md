# Inviting Family Members

Share the tending with those who knew them best. Every perspective makes the story richer.

---

## How to Invite Someone

### Step-by-Step

1. **Open Branch Settings**

   Navigate to the branch you want to share and click the settings icon (‚öôÔ∏è) in the top right corner.

2. **Navigate to Sharing Tab**

   You'll see tabs: Details, **Sharing**, Heirs. Click Sharing.

3. **Click "Invite Someone"**

   Enter their email address. They'll need to create a Firefly Grove account if they don't have one.

4. **Choose Permission Level**

   Select what they can do:
   - **View Only**: Read memories, see photos, listen to recordings
   - **Can Add**: View + add new memories and photos
   - **Can Edit**: View + Add + edit existing memories (including ones you wrote)

5. **Add a Personal Message (Optional)**

   Include a note explaining why you're inviting them:

   *"I'm creating a collection of Grandma's stories. Would you add your memories of her too?"*

6. **Send Invitation**

   They'll receive an email with a direct link to join the branch.

---

## Permission Levels Explained

### View Only
- See all memories in the branch
- View photos and listen to audio
- Cannot add or edit anything
- Good for: Extended family, friends, younger relatives

### Can Add
- Everything in "View Only"
- Add new memories, photos, voice recordings
- Cannot edit memories created by others
- Good for: Close family who knew the person well

### Can Edit
- Everything in "Can Add"
- Edit any memory in the branch (even ones others wrote)
- Delete memories (with caution)
- Good for: Co-owners, immediate family, trusted primary contributors

## Managing Collaborators

### Viewing Current Collaborators

Open Branch Settings ‚Üí Sharing tab to see:
- Who has access
- Their permission level
- When they last contributed

### Changing Permissions

1. Find the collaborator in the list
2. Click their permission level dropdown
3. Select new level
4. Changes take effect immediately

### Removing Someone

1. Find the collaborator in the list
2. Click "Remove Access"
3. Confirm removal
4. They'll no longer see the branch in their account

## Tips & Best Practices

- üí° **Send a personal message**
  Generic invites often get ignored. Explain why their perspective matters and what specific memories you hope they'll share.

- üí° **Start with close family, expand later**
  Begin with 2-3 people who knew the person well. Once you've built momentum, invite others.

- üí° **They'll get an email with a direct link**
  The invitation includes a link that takes them straight to the branch. They don't need to navigate your grove structure.

- üí° **Set clear expectations**
  When inviting someone with "Can Edit" permission, let them know the tone and style you're aiming for.

- üí° **Collaboration works best with active contributors**
  One person adding regularly is better than ten people who never participate. Focus on people who are genuinely interested.

## Branch-Level Sharing vs Tree-Level

**Important**: You share individual **branches**, not entire trees.

Why?
- You might want to share "Grandma's Recipes" with everyone
- But keep "Difficult Memories" private or limited to close family

Each branch can have different collaborators with different permissions.

---

## What Collaborators See

When someone accepts your invitation:
- **They see**: Only that specific branch, its memories, and who else has access
- **They don't see**: Your other trees, branches, or personal memories
- **In their grove**: The shared branch appears in a "Shared with You" section

## Common Questions

**Q: Can collaborators invite other people?**

A: No. Only the branch owner (you) can invite people. This prevents branches from being shared beyond your intended circle.

**Q: What if someone stops contributing? Can I remove them?**

A: Yes, absolutely. Open Branch Settings ‚Üí Sharing ‚Üí Remove Access. Their past contributions remain, but they can no longer access the branch.

**Q: Can I share an entire tree at once?**

A: Not directly. You need to share each branch individually. This gives you fine-grained control over who sees what.

**Q: Do collaborators need a paid subscription?**

A: No! People you invite can participate for free. They only need a paid subscription if they want to create their own trees.

**Q: What happens to their contributions if I remove them?**

A: Their memories, photos, and recordings stay in the branch. You own all content in your branches, even if others contributed it.

**Q: Can I see who wrote each memory?**

A: Yes. Each memory shows the author's name and when it was added.
