# The Nest: Bulk Photo Uploads

Got hundreds of photos to add? The Nest is your staging area. Upload everything at once, then "hatch" photos into memories when you're ready.

---

## How to Use The Nest

### Uploading Photos

1. **Go to The Nest**

   Click "The Nest" in your grove header or navigate to `/nest`.

2. **Upload Photos**

   - **Drag and drop** files into the upload area
   - Or **click to browse** and select multiple files
   - Or **paste** images from your clipboard

   You can upload:
   - JPG, PNG, HEIC (iPhone photos)
   - Multiple photos at once (up to 50 per batch)
   - Photos from your computer, phone, or tablet

3. **Wait for Upload**

   Photos upload in the background. You'll see progress indicators. Once uploaded, they appear in your Nest grid.

---

### Hatching Photos into Memories

**Method 1: Hatch from Nest**

1. Open The Nest (`/nest`)
2. Select one or more photos (checkbox in corner)
3. Click "Hatch from Nest" button
4. Choose the tree and branch
5. Choose how to hatch:
   - **Create new memory for each photo**
   - **Add all photos to one existing memory**
   - **Create one new memory with all photos**

**Method 2: Hatch from Branch**

1. Open a branch
2. Click "Add Memory" or edit an existing memory
3. Click "Add from Nest"
4. Select photos from your Nest
5. Photos are automatically removed from Nest after hatching

## The Hatching Process

When you "hatch" a photo:

1. **Photo moves from Nest â†’ Branch**
2. **Nest copy is deleted** (to avoid duplicates)
3. **Photo becomes part of a memory**
4. **Permanent storage in Firefly Grove**

You can't un-hatch. Once it's part of a memory, removing the photo means deleting it from that memory.

## Nest vs Direct Upload

**Use The Nest when:**
- You have many photos to upload at once
- You're not sure where photos belong yet
- You want to review quality before committing
- You're digitizing old albums

**Upload directly to a memory when:**
- You have 1-3 photos
- You know exactly which memory they belong to
- You're adding photos while writing a memory

Both methods work. The Nest just adds a staging step for bulk operations.

## Common Questions

**Q: How long do photos stay in the Nest?**

A: Forever, until you hatch or delete them. There's no automatic cleanup. We recommend reviewing your Nest monthly.

**Q: Does the Nest count against my storage limit?**

A: Yes. Photos in the Nest count toward your plan's storage limit, just like memories.

**Q: Can I download all photos from my Nest?**

A: Not currently, but you can download individual photos. Use your Forever Kit export if you need a full backup.

**Q: What happens if I delete a photo from the Nest accidentally?**

A: It's permanently deleted. The Nest doesn't have a trash/recycle bin. Be careful with bulk delete.

**Q: Can others see my Nest photos?**

A: No. The Nest is completely private. Only you can see photos there until you hatch them into shared branches.

**Q: Can I edit photos before hatching?**

A: Not within Firefly Grove. Edit photos on your device before uploading, or after hatching them into memories.

**Q: What file size is too large?**

A: Maximum file size is 20MB per photo. Most phone photos are 2-5MB, which is perfect. If you have large RAW files, export them as JPG first.
